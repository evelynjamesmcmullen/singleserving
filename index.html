Gopenhagen

A day in the capital of Denmark where you don't have to crowd around the little mermaid statue.

EAT

Something Millenial

If you're in the mood for something quasi-healthy and easy on the eyes, definitely head over to Atelier September on Gothersgade 30. They have great coffee, and the Avocado toast- though a typical choice- gets major brownie points for its interesting ingredients, like lemon and chives.

Something Traditional

Denmark is known for its smørrebrod, which are open-faced sandwiches stacked to the high heavens with anything from shrimp and egg to chicken salad. For a true smørrebrod experience, head over to Restaurant Schønnemann for a fried plaice smørrebrod (it's a norweigan fish and I know it sounds weird but once you try it with the remoulade you'll be in love) While you're at it- try the chicken tartelette too!

Something Quick but Still Bomb

Baked fresh every day, Meyer's Bageri has the best bread I have ever had- and that is coming from a self-proclaimed bread connoisseur. They will give you a fresh bun with a cheese of your choice (try the gouda), and it is heavenly.

SHOP

Weekday

Weekday is a Swedish brand, but it has gained prominence in many of its neighboring Scandinavian countries, including Denmark. They focus on cool minimalist streetwear, but also come out with some fun statement pieces as well. The best part about it is that you can't purchase anything from them from the US, so nobody will jack your style :-)

Zara Home

We all know Zara, but what is Zara Home? The answer is a magical place that does not yet exist in the United States that includes any piece of fun home decor that you could ever conjure up in your mind. I'm talking monocrhomatic cutlery, skull candles, giant fur pillows, the works.

Monki

Another European Staple, Monki is like a scandinavian Forever 21, but more on-trend. We all know that somehow Europe is months ahead in fashion, and Monki is the perfect place to go if you want to stunt on em on a budget.

DO

The Glyptotek
